Flagship case study

5AM Check-in — attendance & recurring-fee product

A self-initiated product I owned end-to-end: from problem framing and requirements to prioritization, delivery and iterative hardening.

Role: Product Owner (end-to-end) Type: Web app (admin + public) Stack: Node/Express · PostgreSQL (Neon) · Vercel Status: Shipped & in use

🎯 Problem

A “wake-up at 5AM” accountability group tracks daily attendance and collects a recurring membership fee. Everything was manual — attendance in chat, fees reconciled by hand in spreadsheets. It was error-prone, time-consuming, and the group could never answer the key question: “who hasn't paid this period?”

👥 Users

Admin (group manager) runs check-ins and approves payments. Members have no accounts — they only use a public page to submit receipts and check their own status. Designing for two very different users with opposite needs (control vs. friction-free access) drove most product decisions.

🧠 Key product decisions (decision → rationale / trade-off)

Removed member logins; admin owns the roster.
Members won't maintain accounts. Importing from Excel + admin-driven check-in removed the biggest adoption barrier — at the cost of self-service, which members didn't need.
Strict 5:00–5:59 window, no make-up check-in.
The product's whole purpose is the habit. Integrity of the streak was prioritized over convenience — a deliberate “opinionated product” choice.
Public payment page with 4-digit phone verification.
Balanced accessibility (no login) against privacy. Members prove identity with the last 4 digits of their phone before any personal data or history is shown; brute-force is rate-limited.
Period-based fee model + “unpaid this period” report.
The highest-value feature. Turned opaque, manual reconciliation into a one-screen answer: who paid / pending / unpaid, with expected-vs-collected totals and one-click reminders.
Backup → archive → restore data lifecycle.
To live within a free-tier database, old records compress into aggregates while lifetime totals and streaks stay continuous — a constraint-driven design that protects the metric users care about.

🔁 Process & iterations

1
Discovery

Framed the real pain (manual fee reconciliation) and the success metric (zero “who-paid?” ambiguity).

2
MVP

Attendance + receipt submission + admin approval. Shipped the thinnest end-to-end slice first.

3
Structured review rounds

Repeated “what's missing / what's risky?” passes; each finding triaged into P0 / P1 / P2 and delivered by priority.

4
High-value bets

Period-based fee tracking + dues report + reminders — the features that moved the core metric.

5
Hardening

Security, data-integrity, automated tests and operational tooling before scaling usage.
